> The author argues that writing a compiler is more approachable than traditional textbooks like the Dragon Book suggest. He recommends two practical resources: Jack Crenshaw's "Let's Build a Compiler!" tutorial series for hands-on single-pass compilation, and "A Nanopass Framework for Compiler Education," which advocates breaking compilation into many small, simple transformation passes instead of complex monolithic steps.
