### Coding, System Design, Low Level Design and Behavioral Interviews

Over the past 7 years, I’ve given **80+ interviews** and cleared interviews at multiple big tech companies including **Adobe**, **Amazon**, **Google** and **Microsoft**.

During my interview preparation, I came across **countless resources** (both free and paid ones).

In this article, I will share the **best resources** (most free, some paid) I used to prepare for **coding**, **system design**, **low level design** and **behavioral** interviews.

## 1\. Coding Interviews

### 1.1 Books

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vn36!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa0bbc784-ee8f-4b1b-aa33-56286a223aad_1456x988.webp)

- **[Data Structures and Algorithms Made Easy](https://www.amazon.com/dp/819324527X/):** This was the first DSA book I read. It helped me grasp the fundamental concepts through actual interview problems. It’s a beginner friendly book and available in multiple programming languages.
- **[Cracking the Coding Interview](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0984782850/):** This book contains 189 high quality coding interview problems and their solutions. Solving each problem taught me something new.

### 1.2 YouTube Playlists

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tdoy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F91a5d4eb-0179-497e-a3bb-8638636d7c15_1456x662.webp)

- **[Abdul Bari’s Algorithms Playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLDN4rrl48XKpZkf03iYFl-O29szjTrs_O):** Abdul Bari Sir’s Algorithms playlist is a must-watch for anyone who wants to develop a strong foundation in Algorithms.
- **[Tushar Roy’s Dynamic Programming Playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLrmLmBdmIlpsHaNTPP_jHHDx_os9ItYXr):** This playlist helped me understand how to think about and solve DP problems using bottoms up approach.
- **[William Fiset’s Data Structure Playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLDV1Zeh2NRsB6SWUrDFW2RmDotAfPbeHu):** William explains data structure through visualizations and animations which makes understanding difficult concepts lot easier.
- **[William Fiset’s Graphs Playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLDV1Zeh2NRsDGO4--qE8yH72HFL1Km93P):** This playlist explains all the graph algorithms you need to know for coding interviews.

### 1.3 Courses

- **[Algorithms Part I](https://www.coursera.org/learn/algorithms-part1) & [Algorithms Part II](https://www.coursera.org/learn/algorithms-part2) on Coursera:** Professor **Robert Sedgewick** made learning data structures and algorithms fun and easy through this course. Highly recommend going through this course to understand fundamental data structures and algorithms in depth.
	- **Note:** You can watch all the lectures for free by **auditing** the course. You can skip the Advanced Sections in Algorithms 2 since they are rarely asked.

### 1.4 Practice

- **[LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problemset/):** Learning concepts is not enough. You need to practice what you learned to fully grasp DSA concepts and the best platform to do that is **LeetCode**. If you have limited time, make sure to solve these curated list of problems:
	- **[Blind 75](https://leetcode.com/discuss/general-discussion/460599/blind-75-leetcode-questions)**
		- **[Top Interview 150](https://leetcode.com/studyplan/top-interview-150/)**
		- **[Top 100 Liked](https://leetcode.com/studyplan/top-100-liked/)**

## 2\. System Design Interviews

### 2.1 Books

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ckP7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa5ebc44b-2797-4154-a807-3dd550446ada_1200x630.jpeg)

- **[Designing Data Intensive Applications](https://www.amazon.com/dp/1449373321):** Reading this book helped me understand what goes behind designing large scale systems. It’s a must read if you want to understand how distributed systems work in depth.

### 2.2 YouTube Playlists

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4FDa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd706bb72-57a7-46db-96fb-3cb38499f082_4872x1224.png)

- **[Tech Dummies](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLkQkbY7JNJuBoTemzQfjym0sqbOHt5fnV):** This playlist explains commonly asked System Design Interview Problems in detail. If I can recommend only one channel for learning System Design, it’s this.
- **[CodeKarle](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLhgw50vUymycJPN6ZbGTpVKAJ0cL4OEH3):** This channel has limited videos but each one is filled with insights you won’t find elsewhere.

### 2.3 Courses

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9nnr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0da54fb2-96ea-48eb-b24b-8eb142838811_904x387.png)

- **[Grokking Modern System Design Interview (Educative.io)](https://www.educative.io/courses/grokking-modern-system-design-interview-for-engineers-managers?aff=BZyO):** This course contains System Design fundamental concepts and commonly asked interview questions.I went through this course multiple times and it helped me learn various patterns related to System Design Interviews.

## 5\. Mock Interviews

Solving a problem in an interview is quite different from solving it in the comfort of your home.

That’s why you should try to practice in an interview setting before your actual interview.

I attended **[LeetCode virtual contests](https://leetcode.com/contest/)** and gave mock interviews on **[Pramp](https://www.pramp.com/#/)** few weeks before my interviews.

This made me more confident and improved the speed at which I approached interview problems.

## 6\. Taking Notes

#### Notion

Notion is one of my favorite applications.

For interviews, I created a Notion page and added all the resources and material I need to cover.

I used it to bookmark, track and organize information so that I can easily find it later.
